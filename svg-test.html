Wildlife · In Scene

Coastal composition — pelican above water, dolphin below, turtle in shallows, heron wading, crab on sand, plover on beach
Verdict: mostly continue, with 2 fixes needed before moving on.

Working well:
  • Pelican (flying) — the M-wings read instantly, the pouch and throat are a nice detail
  • Pelican (on piling) — great composition, piling shadow gives depth
  • Shorebird — tiny but reads as a plover, legs + beak + body all clear
  • Dolphin fin — water line stroke makes it sit on the surface perfectly
  • Dolphin jumping — motion arc line is genius, reinforces the leap
  • Ghost crab — cream body against sand works, eye dots sell the character

Two fixes to request from Gemini before next batch:
  1. Heron color collision — you shipped it in #4a7591, which is our water color. Against water the heron vanishes (see the blue card above). Fix: swap body fill to cream #f1e6cc (Great Egret — common in NC marshes) or a muted gray like #7a756d (Great Blue Heron).
  2. Sea turtle perspective mismatch — the spec said "side profile, swimming" but this reads as top-down view of a shell. Side profile would show the rounded carapace arching up and down, a head poking forward, and flippers angled. Worth a redo for consistency with the dolphin (which IS side profile).

Everything else is tuned right — continue with Vegetation next once those two are redone.

Wildlife revisions: Egret is perfect — the cream body pops against water, yellow beak sells the species. Sea turtle profile now reads immediately as swimming sideways.

Vegetation highlights:
  • Sabal palmetto — the radiating fronds are a clean silhouette; instantly reads as palmetto

Note on integration: the new sea oats, palmetto, and live oak can replace my hand-drawn versions in the main Masonboro scene — they're all tighter and more cohesive. I'll do that integration once the whole pack is done.

Busy ICW morning — kayaks, SUP, skiff, pontoon, sailboat sharing the waterway
Verdict: all 7 approved. Move on to Structure.

Highlights:
  • Three kayak colors, same silhouette — smart move. Drop-in substitution for the main scene means we can swap my current two-kayak setup for three color variants without re-engineering the animation wrappers.
  • SUP paddler is cleaner than my hand-drawn version. The explicit head-circle + vertical torso + diagonal paddle reads better at small sizes.

Integration preview: once we have the full pack, I'll swap the current two animated kayaks + SUP in the main scene for these cleaner versions. The sailboat could be a slow-crossing distant element in the waterway. Pontoon is day-trip specific — could animate less frequently.

Structure · In Scene

Waterfront composition — boathouse over water, fish shack on bank, docks at three lengths, boat ramp angling in
Verdict: all 6 approved. Move on to Vehicles.

Standouts:
  • Boathouse — 4 stilts, walls, peaked roof, and the shadow-side treatment give it real dimension. Cream roof reads as weathered tin, which is the NC coastal standard. Best structure asset.
  • Three dock lengths (2/4/6 piling) — genius. Same visual language, scales naturally. I can place them at different compositional needs throughout the scene.
  • Boat ramp — triangular slope with skid-mark strokes. Reads as concrete ramp in silhouette context.
  • Fish shack — peaked tin roof + dark door + small window = instantly rustic waterfront. Matches boathouse palette.

Integration impact: once vehicles are done, I'll redo the whole mainland strip in the main scene — boathouse + docks + ramp + fish shack in place of my current simple pilings + truck. Big visual upgrade incoming.
